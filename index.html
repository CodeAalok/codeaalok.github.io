  • Explore and understand the world with Harvard In Focus is a curated examination of Harvard's research, scholarly work, and community. Recent topics include:
    1. Sleep The Harvard community explores this state of rest, its many benefits and functions, and what we can do to ensure that we're getting enough of it. Learn more about sleep
      1. What's inside
        1. He who is awake outside sleeps. He who sleeps awakens within.
        2. Parents may be wrong about teenage sleep
    2. Performing Arts Whether its dance, theater, or musical performance, the Harvard community hits just the right note on all of our campus stages, creating vibrant, innovative experiences for patrons in our neighborhood and around the world. Tread the boards
      1. What's inside?
        1. Life of Pi The American Repertory Theater's theatrical adaptation of Yann Martel's award-winning novel about a zookeeper's teenage son stranded on a lifeboat featured large-scale animal puppets.
        2. Julia Riew There was no Korean Disney princess so Julia Riew created her own for her senior thesis. Since releasing her first video about it, she has received notes and support from all over the world, extensive media coverage, and an agent.
    3. The Great Migration Harvard experts explore the migration of millions of African Americans from the South to urban hubs in the Northeast, Midwest, and West, one of the largest internal migrations in American history. Explore the journey
      1. What's inside?
        1. The exodus on stage An overlooked opera that tells the diaspora story of African Americans, beginning in an unnamed West African village, traveling to a Southern plantation, and ending amid the Harlem Renaissance.
        2. The great frustration Cities that were once beacons of opportunity for Black Americans are now places of poverty and despair. How did it happen?
