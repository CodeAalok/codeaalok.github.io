Trends

Looking at the voting behavior of the Chatra Lok Sabha 2019 and 2014, we see the following trends:

  1. Party affiliation is a game-changer.
  2. National context is emphasized to silence the on-ground issues.
  3. There is little opposition to the incumbent government.
  4. Criminals have a fair chance to win elections.
  5. Women look forward to vote, not to contest.
  6. Money works like magic in politics
  1. Party Dominance
    Candidates with a national party back-up score higher
  2. National Trends
    Emphasis on national context helps silence the region-specific issues
  3. Dispersed Opposition
    Divided Opposition became a great help to the winner
  4. Less or No Woman Participation
    Higher turn-out but less/no women contesting
  5. Criminal Records
    Criminal background seldom matters
  6. Money Power
    Rich
    candidates scored higher
